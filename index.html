FAQ

Your Questions.
Answered.

Still on the fence? Here are the questions I hear most before a first conversation.

Reach Out to Dr. Sharp
Do I need a gym membership to see you?
No. Your session fee covers facility access during our appointment. No existing membership needed at either location.
Do I need a doctor's referral?
No referral needed. Idaho allows direct access to physical therapists, so you can reach out right away. If imaging or a physician evaluation makes sense, I'll help you figure out those next steps.
Why don't you take insurance?
Insurance based physical therapy means shorter sessions, less time with the actual doctor, and a third party deciding what care you get. Working outside of the insurance based system allows me to provide 60 minutes of focused, individualized care every time. No limitations. No shortcuts. No red tape to work around.
Can I use my HSA or FSA?
Yes. Physical therapy is a qualified medical expense. HSA and FSA cards are accepted at the time of service.
How many sessions will I need?
Because sessions are longer and fully one-on-one, most people reach their goals in fewer visits than a traditional clinic. I'll give you a realistic timeline during your first session so you always know what to expect.
I'm not a competitive athlete. Is this for me?
Absolutely. In my eyes, if you want to move without pain and feel good in your body, you're an athlete. Whether you're training for a competition or want to carry groceries without wincing, the same fundamentals apply.
What if I'm in a lot of pain right now?
Right now is a great time to come in. Hands on care and guided movement can help calm the system and reduce symptoms creating a window for recovery.
What's your cancellation policy?
Please give me at least 24 hours notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. I do my best to honor your schedule and just ask for a heads-up if plans change.
